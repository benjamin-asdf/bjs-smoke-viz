Generative smoke, painted entirely by the music — nothing is filmed or
pre-drawn, every frame is simulated.

Under the hood:
 • A spectral fluid solver (Jos Stam's "Stable Fluids", FFT formulation)
   carries the smoke on a periodic grid.
 • The spectrum is mapped to a radial clock: each frequency band continuously
   puffs coloured smoke from its own angle around the circle — bass and treble
   sit on opposite arcs — so the whole image is the live spectrum, blooming
   outward. Quiet tones are lifted so soft passages still breathe smoke.
 • Thousands of Physarum (slime-mould) agents grow a flowing colour network
   AND stir the fluid along their own traces, so the smoke streams down the
   veins they carve.
 • The voice/vocal energy lights a bright source in the centre.
 • Beat onsets gust the wind and kick the simulation's time-step, so the smoke
   lurches and swirls on the hits.
 • The last 30 seconds play with the audio cut — the smoke keeps flowing and
   slowly settles on its own, with no reaction. (My favourite part.)

Built in Clojure + Quil (OpenGL), rendered offline at 1080p.
Code / source:  https://github.com/benjamin-asdf/bjs-smoke-viz
